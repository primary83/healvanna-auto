About the Brand

For Life — Electric

Volvo has committed to becoming a fully electric brand by 2030. The Swedish automaker — now owned by China's Geely — brings its legendary safety expertise to the EV era with advanced driver assistance and robust crash structures.

The EX30 is Volvo's most affordable EV ever, targeting the mass market with Scandinavian design and a sub-$37K price. The EX90 flagship replaces the XC90 with a tech-forward three-row electric SUV featuring LiDAR for safety.

Volvo's minimalist Scandinavian design aesthetic translates beautifully to EVs — clean interiors, sustainable materials, and Google Built-In infotainment create a calming, premium driving experience.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Volvo going all-electric?

+

Volvo has committed to selling only fully electric vehicles by 2030. The EX30 and EX90 represent the new generation, while models like the XC40 Recharge bridge the transition.

Is the Volvo EX30 worth it?

+

The EX30 offers excellent value — Volvo safety, Scandinavian design, and 275 miles of range for $36K. It's one of the most affordable premium EVs on sale, competing with the Kia Niro EV and Chevy Equinox EV.

How safe are Volvo EVs?

+

Extremely safe. Volvo has decades of safety leadership, and the EX90 adds LiDAR for obstacle detection. All Volvo EVs include advanced driver assistance, multiple airbags, and robust crash structures designed for the heavier weight of batteries.
